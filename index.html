This past summer I had the pleasure of working on a Computational Biophysics REU simulating Microtube polarity sorting in the axon. As part of that project I produced some animations and data visualizations which can be found in the links below. This quarter I am joining two other CWU students in completing the assembly of a brand new Spectrograph for CWU's 0.6m research telescope. I will be leading the software front of the project in developing data reduction software and linking the spectrograph to our primary telescope control computer. Outside of research I helped run a summer camp for NESSP and was hired as a guide astronomer for a private rafting trip in August 2023. I am currently working with a private astrophotographer to provide technical support to a newly built 0.6m research telescope and will be maintaining the facility starting January. I am the Safety Officer for the CWU Wildcat Rocketry Team competing in the 2023 NASA Student Launch Challenge and will be leveraging 12 years competetive robotics experience through FIRST.
